## Thesis

The mathematics hidden in what you can touch, hear, and see. Your body, your kitchen,
your neighborhood, the nature around you. Start with the feeling in an elevator. Expand
outward through your senses, your breakfast, the living world, the things humans build
together, the things we find beautiful. End with a glimpse upward.

The organizing principle: start with your own body, expand outward through your kitchen,
your neighborhood, the living world, the things humans build together, the things we find
beautiful, and finally the cosmos — then close with a wave. Read the part titles in
sequence and you'll feel the arc: from the intimate to the infinite, from the known to
the unknown.

**Character:** Warm, surprising, grounded. Most essays resolve. The reader finishes
with new eyes — math is everywhere they look.

## The Storyline in One Sentence

> *You are doing mathematics every time you catch an egg, ride an elevator, blow a
> bubble, watch a starling, hum a tune, stand in line, or gaze at the stars — and
> you already knew it in your bones.*
