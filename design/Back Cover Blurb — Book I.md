# Back Cover Blurb — Book I: *Everything Is a Rate of Change*

You felt it this morning. The lurch in your stomach as the elevator started climbing. The way your cereal clumped together in the bowl. The shower curtain that billowed inward the moment you turned on the water.

You didn't think of any of these as mathematics. But they are — and you already knew the answers in your bones.

*Everything Is a Rate of Change* begins where you stand: in the kitchen, in the shower, at a traffic light. It follows the hidden equations outward — through the hexagons in a beehive, the spots on a leopard, the impossible perfection of a soap bubble — and upward, to the satellites that correct for Einstein's relativity every time your phone recalculates a route.

These 34 essays never ask you to solve a problem. They ask you to notice one. Why does mud crack in patterns? Why can't a piano ever be perfectly in tune? Why does a hanging chain refuse to be a parabola? The mathematics was always there — in your senses, your neighborhood, the living world, and the things humans build when they reach for beauty.

You've been doing mathematics your whole life. This book shows you where.
