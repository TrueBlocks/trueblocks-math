# Plan for Dwarfs Eight Through Fourteen

## Thesis

A darkly comic fairy tale for grown-ups about family, dysfunction, and the lies we tell ourselves. Seven forgotten dwarfs—the second cousins nobody talks about—live in the shadow of their famous relatives, trapped in a compound divided by ancient grudges and modern neuroses. What happens when the fairy tale cameras stop rolling and real life sets in? 

The story unfolds through the fractured perspectives of each dwarf as they navigate their divided household, their complicated relationships with mental health, and the bitter truth that Snow White chose the other guys. Part domestic drama, part psychological study, part absurdist comedy—this is the fairy tale Disney never made, exploring themes of depression, codependency, family trauma, and what it means to be the supporting characters in someone else's happily ever after.

**Character:** Bittersweet, honest, unexpectedly touching. Each dwarf gets their moment of dignity amid the chaos. The reader finishes understanding that even the most dysfunctional families contain multitudes.
