# Back Cover Blurb — Book II: *The Hidden Architecture*

Thirty percent of all naturally occurring numbers begin with the digit 1. Your stock portfolio wanders like a drunk on a number line. A coffee mug and a donut are, to a mathematician, the same object.

Behind every familiar experience — the weather forecast, the grocery checkout line, a coiled seashell on a beach — there is an invisible structure doing most of the work. *The Hidden Architecture* is about learning to see it.

Where *Everything Is a Rate of Change* revealed the mathematics in what you can touch and hear, this second volume goes deeper: into the probability that fools your intuition, the topology that cares about holes rather than shapes, the information theory that measures surprise itself, and the gentle curves that nature draws when no one is watching. A grandmother's crochet turns out to encode hyperbolic geometry. Three strands of yarn contain an algebra. Spider silk solves an optimization problem that would stump an engineering team.

These 37 essays won't teach you to calculate. They'll teach you to recognize the scaffolding — elegant, hidden, and breathtakingly precise — that holds the ordinary world together.
