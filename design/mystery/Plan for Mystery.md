# Plan for Mystery

## Thesis

Three young siblings—Becky, Brandon, and Babe—embark on their annual summer visit to their grandparents in Philadelphia's historic Old City, where their grandfather works as a docent at Carpenter's Hall. What begins as a routine vacation transforms into a thrilling historical mystery when they discover an 18th-century journal written by a boy their age, revealing clues to a long-lost artifact connected to the founding fathers and the secret societies of colonial Philadelphia. As the children follow the trail through the city's neighborhoods, churches, and hidden passages, they uncover family secrets that span generations and find themselves caught between preserving history and protecting a treasure that others desperately want to reclaim.

The central conflict emerges when the children discover the location of the mysterious stolen object—a significant artifact from the colonial era that was taken by children centuries ago and never returned. Modern descendants of the original Masonic families demand its return, but the protagonists must decide whether the artifact belongs in private hands or should be preserved for all of Philadelphia to treasure. Through their adventure, each child grows in courage and independence while learning that family history is more complex and meaningful than they ever imagined.
