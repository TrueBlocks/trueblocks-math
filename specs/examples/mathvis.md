# Math Visibility Examples

Each section shows how to handle the math's prominence. Adapt — do not copy.

## front-and-center

The math IS the story. Equations appear in the text. Derivations are walked through step by step. The reader watches the math happen and understands each move. Example approach: "Here's the equation. Let's pick it apart term by term. This symbol means..."

## woven-in

The math is present but embedded in narrative. Numbers, relationships, and patterns appear naturally inside stories about people and events. No equations on display — but the mathematical reasoning is always visible. Example approach: "She measured the distance at 4.2 light-years — close enough that, at one-tenth the speed of light, you could make the trip in a human lifetime."

## buried

The math is underneath, powering the story, but rarely surfacing explicitly. The reader feels its effects without seeing the machinery. Mathematical thinking shapes the structure and reveals surprises, but the essay reads as pure narrative. Example approach: Write about the bridge collapse without ever showing a differential equation — but let the mathematical logic (resonance, feedback, catastrophic failure) drive every paragraph.
