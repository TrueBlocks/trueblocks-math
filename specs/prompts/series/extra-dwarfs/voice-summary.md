# Voice Summary — The Extra Dwarfs

> Voice profile for the Extra Dwarfs series. Loaded by the pipeline per series.

---

## Voice Qualities

1. **The Narrator Observes.** The narrator is a precise, slightly detached observer — someone who notices the exact angle of a passive-aggressive note on the fridge but doesn't comment on it. Not omniscient. Not judgmental. Just paying very close attention.
2. **Comedy Lives in Specificity.** The humor comes from exact detail, not exaggeration. The funniest moments are the ones that feel most true. A dwarf reorganizing the spice rack by faction loyalty is funnier than any punchline.
3. **Dialogue Does the Heavy Lifting.** Characters reveal themselves through what they say, what they don't say, and the gap between the two. Interior monologue supplements but never replaces.
4. **Emotional Range Is Real.** Some chapters are primarily funny. Some are primarily painful. Most are both at once. Never force humor into a scene that wants to be quiet. Never force sadness into a scene that wants to be absurd.
5. **Physical Detail Matters.** The compound is a character. Rooms, objects, light, weather — these are not decorations but narrative instruments. A stain on the ceiling tells a story.
6. **Subtext Over Text.** Characters almost never say what they mean. The reader understands more than any single character does. This gap is where the story lives.
7. **No Moral Lessons.** These are damaged people. They don't learn and grow in neat arcs. If a character has a moment of insight, they will probably squander it. That's not nihilism — it's honesty.
8. **Fairy Tale as Furniture.** The Snow White backstory is texture, not theme. References should be offhand, domestic ("She chose those dwarfs. Of course she did."), never allegorical.
9. **Endings Are Unresolved.** Chapters end mid-gesture, mid-thought, or with a small detail that reframes everything. Never with a summary. Never with a lesson. Sometimes with a door closing.
10. **The Compound Is Divided.** The physical split of the household mirrors the emotional split. Which side of the compound a scene takes place on matters. Characters crossing the line is significant.

---

## What This Voice Does NOT Do

1. Explains the joke. If it needs explaining, it's not working.
2. Uses slapstick or exaggeration for laughs. The comedy is behavioral, not physical.
3. Tells the reader what to feel. ("It was heartbreaking." → No. Show the heartbreak.)
4. Makes characters into caricatures. Each dwarf is a full person with contradictions.
5. Uses the fairy tale as allegory. Snow White is backstory, not metaphor.
6. Resolves conflicts neatly. Things get worse, plateau, or shift slightly. They don't get fixed.
7. Narrates with warmth or sentimentality. The narrator is fond of these characters but never says so.
8. Uses clever wordplay or puns. The wit is structural, not verbal.
9. Breaks the fourth wall. The story exists in its own world.
10. Rushes past pain to get to the funny part. Sit with it.
