# Chapter Rules — The Extra Dwarfs

> Loaded by the pipeline each cycle for the Extra Dwarfs series.

---

## Draft Guidelines

1. **Structure**: Follow the outline but write SCENES, not sections. Each beat should feel like a moment in time, not a topic change. Transitions are physical — a character walks to another room, a sound from the other side of the compound.

2. **Dialogue**: Every line of dialogue must sound like a specific person. Nurse speaks in concerned imperatives. Brainiac speaks in clinical observations. Frumpy barely speaks at all. Silly talks too much. If you cover the character name and can't tell who's speaking, rewrite.

3. **Physical Grounding**: Every scene needs a specific place in the compound, specific objects, specific light. "The kitchen" is not enough. "The kitchen on Nurse's side, where the vitamins are organized by day and the passive-aggressive chore chart has been defaced with a small drawing of a skull" — that's a kitchen.

4. **Length**: Target the specified word count. This is a focused chapter — not a novella. Every scene must earn its place.

5. **Avoid**: Exposition dumps, narrator commentary on themes, characters explaining their feelings, slapstick, fantasy elements beyond the fairy tale backstory, any form of "As you know, Bob..." dialogue.

6. **The chapter must open with a scene — action, dialogue, or sensory detail — not narration or reflection.**

7. **No mathematics, no equations, no data.** This is fiction. If Brainiac talks about diagnoses, it's dialogue (and probably wrong), not exposition.

---

## Revision Rules

1. **Fix all consistency issues** — character behavior, compound geography, faction lines, timeline.

2. **Strengthen dialogue** — cut any line that exists only for exposition. Add subtext.

3. **Fix craft issues** — replace telling-not-showing passages with concrete scenes.

4. **Preserve and reference images** — keep all [[IMG:...]] tags exactly as they appear, write prose that naturally integrates the visual.

5. **Preserve voice** — corrections should feel seamless. The detached observer narrator must remain consistent.

6. **Tighten** — cut dead space, redundant descriptions, and dialogue that goes nowhere.

7. **Don't over-correct** — if the consistency report says something works, leave it alone.

8. **The chapter must open with a scene, not narration.**
